About

I work on building reliable AI systems that can reason under constraints, respect privacy and operate in real deployments. My recent work explores RL guided alignment, LLM unlearning and privacy, clinical NLP under tight resource limits and multimodal forecasting.

I completed my B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering at IIIT Hyderabad (CGPA 8.29 out of 10, ML CGPA 9.14 out of 10). I have since worked with TCS Research, PayPal Research, Microsoft Research India and collaborators at UCSB and UCSC. A common theme in my work is using post training methods, constrained objectives and careful evaluation to move models from fluent text generation toward consistent, verifiable reasoning.

Research Focus

I am interested in how RL style optimization, structured feedback and learning science can guide large language models toward stable reasoning behaviors instead of only optimizing for surface level fluency.

The limitations of current LLMs became clear to me while working with noisy fraud investigation notes at PayPal and anonymized data for unlearning at TCS Research. In both settings, fluent answers were not enough. The systems had to respect constraints around privacy, consistency and safety, and their behavior had to be measurable and improvable over time.

I now focus on questions such as:

  • How to frame alignment and unlearning as constrained RL problems where rewards explicitly balance privacy, utility and safety.
  • How to design reward functions and feedback signals that encourage correct multi step reasoning rather than only correct final answers.
  • How ideas from learning sciences and instructional design can inform the way we structure feedback, curricula and evaluation for models that support human learning.
